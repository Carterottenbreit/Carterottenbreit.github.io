Arcane Survival Guide for Coworkers

Wizard Types

  1. The Over-Enthusiastic Intern – Young, eager, and dangerously excited about magic. Will accidentally summon a minor demon while trying to “improve team morale.”
  2. The Absent-Minded Archmage – Power level: ridiculous. Memory: nearly nonexistent. Often forgets where they put their staff… usually because it’s floating behind them.
  3. The Coffee Conjurer – Casts Caffeine Surge every hour. Keeps the entire office awake through sheer arcane willpower. Smells like espresso and burnt mana.
  4. The Meeting Teleporter – Why walk to the conference room when you can rip space-time open? Never uses doors. Scares interns. Interrupts Zoom calls by materializing inside them.
  5. The Chaos Bard Adjacent – Not technically a wizard… but close enough. Their presence increases the chance of random musical numbers by 300%.
  6. The Hex-Coder – A software engineer who uses real spells to debug code. Comments in their scripts look like incantations. Sometimes the code literally yells back.
  7. The Prophecy Guy – Constantly predicts minor things like “you're going to spill that coffee.” Is somehow always right. Won’t explain how they know.
  8. The Crystal-Everything Mage – Charges crystals constantly. Has “charging rocks” on their desk, in the washroom, in their car, maybe on the roof. Claims productivity boosts of “+3 Charisma” and “+5 PowerPoint Strength.”
  9. The Familiar-Friendly Wizard – Always accompanied by a magical pet (raven, cat, spectral hamster). HR has repeatedly requested they stop bringing it to all-hands meetings.
  10. The Necromancer With an HR Certificate – Surprisingly good at administrative tasks. Raises dead paperwork from the shredder to finish projects. Has an “Ask Me About Compliance” mug.
  11. The Time-Loop Analyst – Keeps saying “We’ve had this meeting before.” They’re right. They’ve already lived this quarter three times.
  12. The Passive-Aggressive Enchanter – Curses office supplies when upset. Staplers vanish. Pens leak. Printers become hostile and demand tribute.
